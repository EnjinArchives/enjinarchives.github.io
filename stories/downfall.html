Enjin's Downfall

(Originally posted: 2024-02-11)

Although Enjin officially shut down on 30 April 2023, the site had been in a downward spiral for quite a long time. The causes for this are numerous. Some of it is simply owed to economic and social factors, such as the consolidation of web traffic into a small number of social media sites; however, in this article, we will recap the non-economic factors. That is, what was unique to Enjin that went wrong?

The Beginning Of The End: Mineplex's Departure

Mineplex was once the busiest Minecraft server worldwide. It had tens of thousands of users on at all times. Since their website was on Enjin, this was a major source of revenue and traffic for Enjin, and Enjin owed many of its active users to Mineplex's activity. What could go wrong? Well, naturally, when a business is overly dependent on one customer, the risk of running the business is high, as the customer could always leave if, say, the site is a breeding ground for monsters (mostly true in this case). So, what caused Mineplex to leave?

Staff Problems

Mineplex was in its own downward spiral; arguably, this was the first root cause of the end of Enjin. In the mid-2010s, Mineplex dealt with multiple sex abuse scandals, involving staff members taking advantage of users who were minors. Naturally, this causes uproar on Enjin; the "riots" of those times were a response to these incidents.
